Habit: A regular tendency or practice.

To form a habit it takes 66 days on average. They can bring positivity or negativity into your life. They are harder to break, reducing mental resistance to start a healthy task such as exercise or increase mental resistance to quit an unhealthy task such as smoking. Habits are a powerful tool to shape your life, only through consistency and deliberate practice can mastery be achieved. Make the right habits.

Journal: To write in a journal or diary.

The habit of journaling allows for self reflection and contemplation, only through review can one make the most effective decisions. Journaling does more than self-expression and noting memories, there are a wide range of short to long term benefits from reduced stress/anxiety to increasing memory capacity and improving immune functions.

Notebook: A place to make notes.

To make notes, to record information captured from another source for developing knowledge or future reference. By taking notes, the writer captures the essence of the information, freeing their mind from having to recall everything.
